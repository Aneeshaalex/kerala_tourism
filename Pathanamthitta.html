Pathanamthitta is one of the fastest-growing cities in India, and one of the first of its kind in the country to go completely cashless in 2017. As part of a government initiative to support e-wallets, the Pathanamthitta district collectorate office has rejected the use of cash 2017 onwards. This city is also considered the seat of Hindu mythology, since its home to the Sabarimala temple, which attracts millions of faithful devotees every year.

The Sabarimala Temple is a temple complex located at Sabarimala hill inside the Periyar Tiger Reserve in the Perinad Village, Pathanamthitta district, Kerala, India. It is one of the largest annual pilgrimage sites in the world with an estimate of over 40 to 50 million devotees visiting every year.

1.PERUNTHENARUVI
A natural waterfall with captivating surroundings is situated on the banks of the River Pamba. The fall is 60 to 100 ft deep which pours down on a rocky bed. The place is a favourite picnic spot among locals as well as tourists.

2.GAVI
Gavi is situated some 14 km away from the famed Periyar Tiger Reserve and is heaven on Earth, quite literally

3.ARANMULA
Aranmula is a place mainly known for its expert craftsmanship and snake boat race organized on the final day of Onam Festival. The most renowned art of metal mirror of Aranmula Kannadi has its roots originated from this place.

4.KONNI FOREST RESERVE
This place is considered to be the equivalent to an elephant's cage. The reason being is that the main attraction of the region is a massive wooden cage where elephant are trained.
