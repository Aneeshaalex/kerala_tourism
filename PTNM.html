5 Best Places in Pathanamthitta

Pathanamthitta is one of the fastest-growing cities in India, and one of the first of its kind in the country to go completely cashless in 2017. As part of a government initiative to support e-wallets, the Pathanamthitta district collectorate office has rejected the use of cash 2017 onwards. This city is also considered the seat of Hindu mythology, since its home to the Sabarimala temple, which attracts millions of faithful devotees every year. To understand Pathanamthitta, you’d have to look within. And in case you’re wondering what to do once in the Land of Lord Ayyappa besides visiting the Sabarimala temple, here’s our list of the best places to visit in and around Pathanamthitta.

Pandalam Palace

The Pandalam family was an off-shoot of the mighty Pandya dynasty, and in spite of being a small faction, commanded great wealth and respect in what is now modern Kerala, albeit thousands of years ago. The Pandalam Palace is maintained in their memory and contains mixed influences of Pandya and Malabar architecture. The Palace is considered the childhood home of Lord Ayyappa, and is one of the foremost Pathanamthitta tourist places.

Top tip - There are a lot of strict rules you need to follow if you want to visit the Pandalam Palace, so consult someone from the local tourism board.

Aranmula

Aranmula, a UN heritage site, is famous for two things - the boat race and the Aranmula Parthasarathy Temple. Then, you can also buy an Aranmula Kannadi, an intricately-designed metal mirror made using a secret method that’s been passed down from over generations. It’s said to have a healing influence. Next, try and plan your trip during the Vallasadya, which is a huge 70-item feast celebrating the bravery and physical might of the oarsmen who participate in the boat festival. There’s always another story around the corner in Aranmula, making it one of the best places to visit in Pathanamthitta. If you want to get in on the snake boat race, it’s best to make your bookings in advance at one of the many heritage lodges available in the area. Thriveni Sangamam

Hindus believe that rivers originating from holy places have a cleansing effect, and a dip in one will erase all the sins in the past. Now, that depends on how much of a believer you are, but at Thriveni Sangamam, there’s one thing for sure - it’s one of the most breathtaking places to visit in Pathanamthitta. It is a confluence of three rivers - the Pamba River Manimala River and the Achankovil River, all deeply connected with Sabarimala. A dip into the refreshing waters will definitely wipe away your tiredness, and maybe, a few sins here and there. Top tip - Carry your own food and keep your belongings close, as pickpockets are quite common.

Gavi

Gavi is a carefully-protected natural reserve inhabited by some of the rarest and most elusive animals known to man. That’s why you have to be quiet, and carry your own litter when in Gavi. The local authorities, along with the villagers, go to great lengths to maintain this ecological vault of sorts. Gavi is a great place to go to trekking, but you have to be accompanied by forest officials wherever you go. The way the people treat Mother Nature will create deep respect for Gavi, making it one of the top tourist spots in Pathanamthitta.

Konni Forest Reserve

The Konni Forest reserves are a great place to spend a day with your family if you’re visiting Pathanamthitta, especially because it’s a very well-organised forest resort with excellent guides willing to take you around the place. If you can visit the elephant training centre where they break in wild elephants. Or, ride the bowl-shaped boats. Top tip- the bowl boats may get your head spinning, so better ride it on an empty stomach. You wouldn’t want to make a mess of yourself in one of the best places to visit in Pathanamthitta, right?

Yes, most tour guides will swear by a visit to Sabarimala, but it’s off the beaten path that you find real treasure. Pathanamathitta, a jungle kingdom, beckons the young and the old. As long as you’re mindful of the local traditions and don’t litter the place, you will be fine. It’s the same as other tourist places, we guess. Flights to Pathanamathitta are generally overbooked in November and December because the Sabarimala temple is open during this period. But you can also plan a visit sometime just before the peak season, and that is in September or October. It’s when the monsoons wear off and the place starts to cool down, since winter season from November to February. You will need a local guide, which many top hotels will be able to organise for you.
